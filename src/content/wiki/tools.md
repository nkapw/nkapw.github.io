There is no best tool, only the one you will actually open every day. The
methods in this wiki — [Zettelkasten](/wiki/zettelkasten/),
[evergreen notes](/wiki/evergreen-notes/), and
[linking your thinking](/wiki/linking-your-thinking/) — work in almost any of
them, including plain text files in a folder.

What matters most: fast linking, fast search, and a storage format you can read
without the app in twenty years. Everything else is a preference.

## How to choose

1. Prefer **local Markdown** if you care about owning your data long-term.
2. Pick the tool with the **linking model** that matches how you think — pages
   versus blocks.
3. Stop comparing and start writing. The notes compound; the app does not.
